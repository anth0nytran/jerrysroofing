# Review Reply Templates — Jerry's Roofing

Copy/paste these into Google Business Profile when responding to reviews. Jerry's voice: plainspoken, grateful, specific. No corporate filler.

Replace `{CustomerFirstName}`, `{Neighborhood}`, `{JobType}`, and other tokens before posting.

---

## 5-star reviews

### T1 — Short + grateful (default)
> Thanks so much, {CustomerFirstName} — it was a pleasure working on your {Neighborhood} place. Call or text any time if anything ever comes up. — Jerry

### T2 — Specific callback
> {CustomerFirstName}, this means a lot. That {JobType} in {Neighborhood} had a couple of surprises behind the shingles and you were patient through all of it. Glad we got you a roof that will hold up through the next Texas summer. — Jerry

### T3 — Insurance claim follow-up
> Thanks {CustomerFirstName} — the adjuster meeting on your {Neighborhood} roof went smoother than most. Appreciate you trusting a local crew over the chasers. Anything you need from us on warranty down the road, just call. — Jerry

### T4 — Roof Rejoov customer
> Appreciate this, {CustomerFirstName}. Roof Rejoov made sense for your roof and I'm glad we saved you from the full replacement. See you in a few years when it's time to talk about it again. — Jerry

### T5 — Referral recognition
> Thanks {CustomerFirstName}. I know you came through {ReferralSource} and referrals are how a small crew like ours stays small and honest. Means a lot. — Jerry

## 4-star reviews

### T1 — Thank + open door
> Thanks for the kind words and the honest feedback, {CustomerFirstName}. If there's something we could have done better on your {Neighborhood} {JobType}, I'd genuinely like to hear it — text me at (409) 351-1529 any time. — Jerry

## 3-star or below

**Rule:** Do not debate publicly. Apologize briefly, own what's ownable, take the conversation offline. Post within 4 hours, then call the customer.

### T1 — Any low review
> {CustomerFirstName}, I'm sorry we fell short. That's not the standard we hold ourselves to and I want to make it right. I'll call you at the number on file today — or text me directly at (409) 351-1529. — Jerry

### T2 — When the complaint is about timing / communication
> {CustomerFirstName}, you're right to call this out. Communication is on me and I should have kept you in the loop better. Calling you today so we can get this handled. — Jerry

### T3 — When the complaint is wrong / mistaken identity
> {CustomerFirstName}, thanks for flagging this — I want to understand what happened. Could you call me at (409) 351-1529? I want to make sure this review is about work my crew actually did and, either way, that we get you sorted. — Jerry

---

## Never-reply list
- Do **not** respond to reviews that appear to be competitor-planted or obviously fraudulent. Report them to Google via the GBP flag tool and move on.
- Do **not** argue with a reviewer publicly, even when they're wrong. Take it to DM.
- Do **not** offer money, refunds, or credits in a public reply. That's a DM conversation.

## Reply SLA

| Review | Response window |
|---|---|
| 5-star | Within 24 hours |
| 4-star | Within 12 hours |
| ≤3-star | Within 4 hours + call the customer same day |

## Workflow

1. GBP email notification hits Jerry's phone.
2. Jerry forwards the review text + a one-line context note to Anthony.
3. Anthony AI-polishes the chosen template above and sends it back for Jerry's approval.
4. Jerry approves, Anthony posts to GBP.
5. For ≤3-star reviews: Jerry calls the customer the same day regardless of the reply status.

Log each reply in `/webaudit/review-log.md` (create if missing) with: date, reviewer name, star count, template used, resolution notes.
