## Patterns of the Top 10%

After analyzing the 5 top-performing competitors, these patterns separate elite from average:

### Pattern 1: Trust Signals Above the Fold
Every top site puts reviews, certifications, and years of experience in the hero section — not buried in a footer or "About" page. Amstill leads with "1,200+ 4.9-Star Reviews" right below their headline. Jerry's Roofing has ZERO social proof above the fold.

### Pattern 2: Interactive Estimation Tools
The best sites (Onit with Roofr, 314 with cost calculator, Mighty Dog with Roofle) give visitors an immediate, interactive way to get a ballpark price. This reduces friction and captures leads before they leave. Jerry's has no interactive tools.

### Pattern 3: Manufacturer Certifications as Trust Anchors
Top competitors hold 3-4 manufacturer certifications (GAF Master Elite, Owens Corning Platinum, CertainTeed Master Shingle Applicator). Jerry's lists manufacturer logos (IKO, CertainTeed, GAF) but doesn't display certification levels.

### Pattern 4: Location-Specific Landing Pages
Driven Roofing has 15+ city-specific pages. Onit has location pages. This drives local SEO hard. Jerry's mentions service areas in a paragraph — no dedicated pages.

### Pattern 5: Before/After + Project Gallery with Context
Top sites show project galleries with descriptions, locations, and scope details — not just photos. This builds credibility for specific job types.
