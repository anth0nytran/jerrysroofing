Your Part

What You Need to Do to Make This Work

We build the website, set up your GBP, write the blog posts, and handle the technical SEO. But there are things only you can do — and they make or break whether this works.

Post on Your Google Business Profile Every Week

This is the single most impactful thing you can do yourself. Google rewards businesses that post regularly on their profile — and most of your competitors don't do this consistently. It's a wide open advantage.

Aim for 1-2 posts per week. They don't need to be perfect. A photo from a job site + a few sentences is all it takes. Here are the types of posts to rotate through:

Post Type 1: Project Showcase (Your Best Post Type)

1-2x per week

Take a photo of a finished (or in-progress) project. Write 2-3 sentences about what you did and where. Always mention the city. Always end with your phone number or "call for a free estimate."

Example Post
"Just finished removing a 60-foot dead oak in Kingwood, TX. The homeowner had been worried about it falling on their roof for months. Safe removal, full cleanup, same day. Need a tree removed? Call for a free estimate: (713) 291-6992"
Example Post
"Before & after on this tree trimming job in Atascocita. Crown thinning and deadwood removal to keep this family's live oaks healthy and their yard safe. 25+ years experience in every cut. (713) 291-6992"
Example Post
"Stump grinding done in Spring, TX! Ground down 4 stumps from trees that came down in last month's storm. Yard is ready for sod and landscaping. Call today for your free quote."

Post Type 2: Seasonal / Timely

1-2x per month

Tie your services to the time of year. Storm season and spring are your biggest opportunities — get ahead of it.

Example Post
"Hurricane season starts June 1st. Now is the time to get your trees trimmed and deadwood removed before the storms hit. We offer free estimates for homeowners in Humble, Kingwood & Atascocita. (713) 291-6992"
Example Post
"Spring is here — time to clean up your yard! We do tree trimming, mulching, flower bed installation, and full landscaping. Licensed, insured, 25+ years. Call us today."

Post Type 3: Trust Builder

1-2x per month

Remind people why they should hire you. Your experience, your quality, your repeat customers.

Example Post
"25+ years serving Humble and the greater Houston area. Most of our work comes from repeat customers and neighbors who saw what we did next door. Licensed, insured, and ready to work. (713) 291-6992"
Example Post
"Thank you to the Rodriguez family in Summerwood for the 5-star review! We love helping families keep their trees healthy and their yards beautiful."
Why this matters: Google Business posts show up directly in search results when people find your profile. They also signal to Google that your business is active, which improves your local ranking. The competitors who rank highest (ABC Home, Top Choice, Monster Tree) keep their profiles active. The ones that don't sit on page 2.

Quick rules for every post:

  • Always include a photo (phone photos are fine — real beats polished)
  • Always mention the city or neighborhood (Humble, Kingwood, Atascocita, Spring, Summerwood, etc.)
  • Always mention the service (tree trimming, tree removal, stump grinding, storm cleanup, etc.)
  • Always end with your phone number or "call for a free estimate"
  • Keep it 2-4 sentences. Nobody reads a novel on Google.

Ask for Reviews After Every Job

Your review gap is the biggest thing holding you back. You have 50 reviews at 5.0 stars — the quality is perfect. But ABC Home has 800+ and Top Choice has 350+. Google's local ranking algorithm weighs review count AND how recently they were posted.

We'll set up a review follow-up system that makes this easy — a simple link you text or email to the customer after a job. But you need to actually send it.

How to ask (it's simpler than you think)

After a job is done and the customer is happy, send them a text like:

"Hey [Name], thanks for trusting us with your [tree trimming / tree removal / etc]. If you're happy with the work, a Google review would really help us out. Here's the link: [your Google review link]"

That's it. No follow-up needed. Most happy customers will do it if you make it easy.

Goal: 5-8 new reviews per month. At that rate you hit 70+ in 3 months and 100+ in 6 months — that puts you past Green Forest and Mike's, and into striking distance of Monster Tree.

Respond to Every Google Review

When a customer leaves a review, reply within a day or two. Doesn't need to be long. Google tracks whether businesses respond — and customers read the responses.

Response Template
"Thank you [Name]! It was great working on your [tree trimming / removal / stump grinding] in [city]. We appreciate you trusting us with your trees. Enjoy your yard!"
Response Template
"Thanks so much [Name]! Jobs like this are why we love what we do. If you ever need anything in the future — trimming, removal, storm cleanup — don't hesitate to call."

Send Us Project Photos

Every time you finish a project, snap a few photos on your phone and text or email them to us. Include what you did and the city/neighborhood. We use these for:

  • Updating your website gallery
  • Blog post content
  • Your monthly content updates

Takes 2 minutes. Before, during, and after shots are best. Phone photos are fine.

Answer Leads Fast

When a quote request comes in from the website, you'll get an email with the customer's name, phone number, service needed, and timeline. Call them back within an hour if you can. Businesses that respond within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to close. Your competitors are not fast at this — you can win just by picking up the phone.
